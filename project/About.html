About us

Built for foreigners
navigating Japan's job market

Kakehashi (橋) means "bridge" in Japanese. That's exactly what we're trying to be — a direct bridge between foreign talent and Japanese employers who want to hire them without an agency in the middle.

The problem we're solving

Japan has tens of thousands of foreign residents — many of them ALTs, English instructors, or entry-level workers — who want to transition into careers that match their actual skills. But the job search landscape in Japan is stacked against them:

What we offer
  • Direct-hire roles only — no agency middlemen
  • Listings from BOEs, government, and real companies
  • Japanese descriptions translated to English
  • Visa sponsorship clearly labelled
  • MHLW-compliant salary and hours disclosure
  • Up-to-date listings, sourced from official sources
The current landscape
  • Dominated by dispatch agencies taking a cut
  • Job descriptions entirely in Japanese
  • Visa sponsorship buried or unclear
  • Salary ranges vague or missing
  • Listings months out of date
  • No translation or guidance for foreign applicants

Our principles

1
Transparency over convenience
Every listing shows salary, hours, and contract type upfront. No bait-and-switch. If an employer won't provide that information, they don't list with us.
2
No middlemen, no hidden fees
We will never charge candidates a placement fee. When we eventually enable employer listings, those fees will be published openly. The hiring relationship is between you and the employer — we stay out of it.
3
Privacy by default
We don't collect your CV, your contact details, or your application history. You apply directly on the employer's site. Your data is yours.
4
Accuracy over volume
We'd rather show 20 verified, up-to-date listings than 2,000 stale ones. Every listing is manually reviewed or algorithmically verified against a live source before it appears.

Who it's for

  • ALTs (Assistant Language Teachers) looking to transition out of teaching into corporate roles
  • Foreign nationals on Instructor or Engineer visas seeking a change of status
  • Entry-level workers who want roles that don't require near-native Japanese
  • Experienced bilingual professionals seeking Japan-based roles with visa support
  • Anyone tired of wading through agency-dominated job boards with zero transparency

Roadmap

Phase 1 — Complete
Community preview
Static job board with curated listings, filters, bilingual descriptions, and MHLW-compliant data display.
Phase 2 — Now
Live API integration
Connect to Hello Work feeds, BOE official portals, and verified company career pages. Auto-translate Japanese listings via DeepL API. Show source freshness timestamps.
Phase 3 — Next
Resource hub
Guides on converting classroom experience to a Japanese CV (履歴書 / 職務経歴書), JLPT study resources, and visa change-of-status walkthroughs.
Phase 4 — Future
MHLW-licensed placement
Apply for a Fee-Charging Employment Placement licence (有料職業紹介事業許可) to offer opt-in candidate matching for employers who want it. Fully transparent fees.
