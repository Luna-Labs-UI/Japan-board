MHLW Compliance

How Direct Hire in Japan aligns with Japan's Ministry of Health, Labour and Welfare (厚生労働省) job listing standards — and what that means for every role you see on this platform.

Last updated: April 29, 2026
Our Commitment

Every listing on Direct Hire in Japan is verified to include the MHLW-mandated disclosures: salary, working hours, and contract renewal criteria. We remove or flag any listing that does not meet this standard.

What is the MHLW?

The Ministry of Health, Labour and Welfare (MHLW / 厚生労働省) is Japan's government body responsible for labour policy, employment standards, and worker protection. It oversees job advertising regulations under the Employment Security Act (職業安定法), which was significantly updated in 2022 to tighten accuracy requirements on job postings.

Key requirements under the revised Act include mandatory disclosure of working conditions before and at the time of hiring, prohibition of misleading job advertisements, and stricter accountability for both employers and any intermediaries (including job boards).

What must every job listing include?

Under MHLW guidelines, any job advertisement must clearly state the following before a candidate applies:

Required field Details Status on this site
Wages / Salary Minimum and maximum monthly pay, or fixed salary range ✓ Always shown
Working hours Core hours, start/end times, flex arrangements ✓ Always shown
Contract type Full-time, fixed-term, part-time; renewal criteria for contracts ✓ Always shown
Place of work City and prefecture of the role ✓ Always shown
Job description Accurate description of duties — no vague or misleading language ✓ Required
Employer identity The actual hiring organisation must be identified ✓ Required

Listings that cannot be verified against these fields are not published on Direct Hire in Japan.

Our role as a listing aggregator

Direct Hire in Japan is a listing aggregator — we curate and display job ads sourced from publicly available employer websites, official government portals (Hello Work / ハローワーク), and Board of Education recruitment pages. We are not a licensed employment placement agency (有料職業紹介事業者).

Important Distinction

Because we do not match candidates with employers, collect application data, or charge fees for placement, we operate outside the licensing requirements of the Fee-Charging Employment Placement Business framework. We do not mediate the hiring relationship.

All applications go directly to the employer or their official portal. We play no role in shortlisting, interviewing, or hiring decisions.

Visa sponsorship transparency

Japan's immigration and labour laws interact closely. Foreign nationals require a valid Certificate of Eligibility (COE) and appropriate visa status to work legally. Direct Hire in Japan makes the following commitments regarding visa information:

  • Listings marked Visa sponsored explicitly state the employer's willingness to sponsor a work visa or change of status
  • We do not guarantee visa approval — that is the sole jurisdiction of the Immigration Services Agency of Japan
  • Listing descriptions include the visa category where known (e.g., Instructor, Engineer/Specialist, Skilled Labour)
  • Employers are responsible for the accuracy of their visa sponsorship claims

No-agency policy

Direct Hire in Japan specifically focuses on direct-hire roles — positions where the employer, not a staffing or dispatch agency, is the contracting party. This is important for foreign workers in Japan because:

  • Agency contracts (特定派遣 / 一般派遣) often restrict visa sponsorship options
  • Direct employment provides greater stability and access to benefits such as shakai hoken (social insurance)
  • Board of Education direct contracts (直接雇用) carry different rights than agency-dispatched ALT contracts
  • Direct employers are typically more accountable for working condition disclosures

We actively exclude listings from staffing agencies and dispatch companies. If you encounter a listing that appears to be agency-sourced, please report it to us.

Reporting inaccurate listings

Found a problem?

If a listing contains misleading salary information, outdated details, or appears to violate MHLW advertising standards, email us at listings@directhireinjapan.com. We review and respond within 3 business days.

Official MHLW resources

For official guidance on employment rights in Japan, visit the MHLW English portal or your nearest Hello Work (ハローワーク) office.

Future licensing plans

As Direct Hire in Japan grows, we intend to apply for a Fee-Charging Employment Placement Business licence (有料職業紹介事業許可) from the MHLW. This licence requires:

  • A physical office (virtual offices are not accepted)
  • ¥5,000,000 in net assets
  • Designated personal information handling manager
  • Approval process of approximately 2–3 months

Until that licence is obtained, Direct Hire in Japan operates strictly as an information aggregator and does not engage in placement activities.
